Credit Risk Simulation

Modeling Loss Distribution of a BBB Bond Portfolio

üìå Project Overview

This project models the credit risk of a bond portfolio consisting of BBB-rated bonds from two counterparties. It uses two correlation structures‚Äî(A) multivariate Gaussian and (B) Gaussian copula‚Äîto simulate 1,000,000 future portfolio values, estimate expected credit loss, and evaluate the 99% loss percentile.

üß† Objective

The goal is to assess how the dependence structure between counterparties impacts risk predictions. Additionally, the simulation is extended to larger portfolios of 10 and 100 bonds to analyze scaling behavior of credit risk under correlated defaults.

‚öôÔ∏è Methodology

  • Input Data: Marginal rating transition probabilities and valuation matrix for bond outcomes (AAA to Default).
  • Approach A: Simulate joint outcomes using a multivariate Gaussian distribution with 0.5 correlation.
  • Approach B: Simulate using a Gaussian copula with the same correlation structure.
  • Simulations: For each setup, 1 million scenarios were generated to compute:
    • Expected credit loss
    • 99th percentile of the loss distribution
  • Extension: Scale portfolio size to 10 and 100 bonds and plot 99% loss vs. number of bonds.

üìà Results Summary

  • The Gaussian copula structure predicted a higher tail risk than the multivariate Gaussian.
  • Loss percentiles increased with portfolio size but at a decreasing rate due to diversification.
  • Results were visualized through loss distribution histograms and percentile plots.
